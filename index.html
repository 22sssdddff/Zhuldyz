Understanding Virality in Social Media

Platform, Content, and Emotional Drivers

Zhuldyz Askarbekkyzy

What Makes a Post Go Viral?

Social media plays a big role in today’s communication, but only a few posts go truly viral. This project explores what makes a post viral by analyzing 5,000 top-performing posts from TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Twitter.

It focuses on platform differences, content types, and emotions to understand which factors drive views, likes, shares, and comments.

Dataset Overview

This project analyzes a dataset of 5,000 viral social media posts collected from TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Twitter. Each post includes key engagement data — such as views, likes, shares, and comments — along with its main hashtag, content type, and platform.

The dataset covers trending topics like fitness, education, challenges, comedy, and more. By focusing on the top-performing posts, the analysis explores:

  • How different platforms support virality
  • Which hashtags and content types are most effective
  • How engagement metrics relate to each other

In addition, a smaller subset of 500 posts was examined for emotional tone, helping to understand how emotions like joy, anger, or love contribute to virality.
